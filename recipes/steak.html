The Best Steak Marinade

This easy steak marinade is great for rib steaks but can be used for almost any cut of beef. This easy steak marinade is truly the best you'll ever try. Made with convenient ingredients that may already be in your pantry, you'll come back to this steak marinade recipe time after time.


    Easy Steak Marinade Ingredients

    You likely already have all the ingredients you'll need to make our easy steak marinade recipe on hand. If not, here's what to add to your grocery list:

  • Olive oil: This incredibly easy steak marinade starts with olive oil.
  • Balsamic vinegar: Balsamic vinegar adds tangy flavor and helps tenderize the meat.
  • Sauces: You'll need Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and Dijon mustard.
  • Seasonings: The steak marinade gets more flavor from fresh garlic, salt, and pepper.

How to Make Easy Steak Marinade

It truly couldn't be easier to make this quick steak marinade. Just mix all the ingredients together in a small bowl. That's it! You'll find the full, step-by-step instructions in the recipe below.

How to Marinate Steak

Pour the marinade into a resealable plastic bag. Add the steak, coat with the marinade, squeeze out the excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least two hours. Remove steak from the marinade and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade. Cook or grill steaks to desired doneness.

How Long to Marinate Steak

Marinate the steak in the refrigerator for at least two hours. However, for even more flavorful and juicy results, you can marinate the steak for up to eight hours.

Can You Use the Leftover Steak Marinade?

If you don't want to discard the marinade, you can use it to make a sauce to go with your steak. But there's one big caveat: You must boil the marinade for at least five minutes to make it safe to eat. You can thicken it up with a teaspoon of cornstarch, if you'd like.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • ¼ cup Worcestershire sauce
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 pinch salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Mix olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, and garlic in a small bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
